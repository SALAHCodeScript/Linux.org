			   Demonstrative
------------------------------------------------------------------
Demonstratives show where an object, event, or person is in 
relation to the speaker, They can refer to a physical or a 
psychological closeness or distance, When talking about events, 
the near demonstratives are often used to refer to the present 
while the far demonstratives often refer to the past
------------------------------------------------------------------
		       'This' and 'These'
We use this (singular) and these (plural) as pronouns:
To talk about people or things near us:

Example:
	This is a nice cup of tea
	Whose shoes are these?

To introduce people:
Example:
	This is Janet
	These are my friends John and Michael

We say, This is John and this is Michael, (NOT These are John 
	and Michael)

To begin a conversation on the phone:
Example:
	Hello, this is David, Can I speak to Sally?
_________________________________________________________________
		        'That' and 'Those'
We use that (singular) and those (plural) as pronouns to talk 
about things that are not near us:

Example:
	What's that?
	Those are very expensive shoes
	This is our house, and that's Rebecca's house over there

We also use that to reply to something someone has said:
Example:
	'Shall we go to the cinema?' 'Yes, that’s a good idea'
	'I've got a new job' 'That's great'
	'I'm very tired' 'Why is that?'
_________________________________________________________________
	   		    Nouns
We can also use this, these, that and those with nouns, We use 
this and these for people or things near us:

Example:
	We have lived in this house for twenty years
	Have you read all of these books?

and that and those for people or things that are not near us:
Example:
	Who lives in that house?
	Who are those people?
